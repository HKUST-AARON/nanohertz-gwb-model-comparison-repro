Bayesian Evidence for Angular Symmetry and Spectral Curvature in the Nanohertz Gravitational-Wave Background

Hua Xu, Weiming Zhang, and Yike Guo

What does this paper show?

The public NANOGrav 15-year Hellings-Downs free-spectrum evidence favors spectra with physical curvature or a characteristic scale over the canonical scale-free supermassive black hole binary spectrum. Among the tested physical templates, a first-order phase transition has the largest compressed spectral evidence, while environmental SMBHB hardening is the leading astrophysical interpretation.

Key Bayesian evidence results

The canonical gravitational-wave-driven SMBHB power law is the reference model. Values are computed from the corrected public NANOGrav 15-year HD free-spectrum KDE under the baseline priors.

Angular symmetry

The Hellings-Downs correlation fixes the tensor angular-correlation class of the isotropic stochastic background.

Spectral curvature

The evidence favors a free slope or a physical spectrum carrying a bend, peak, or characteristic scale.

Multi-band continuation

The LISA forecast separates a PTA-selected QCD-scale phase transition from an unchanged broad cosmic-string extrapolation.

What can researchers use this work for?

  • Classify astrophysical and cosmological origins of a Hellings-Downs-correlated PTA signal.
  • Connect a low-frequency SMBHB turnover to parsec-scale nuclear stellar environments.
  • Compare first-order phase-transition and effective cosmic-string spectra in one evidence framework.
  • Test PTA-to-LISA spectral consistency and identify models requiring an inter-band break.
  • Benchmark prior sensitivity, low-bin ablations, leave-one-frequency-out stability, and alternative KDE products.
  • Reuse machine-readable evidence tables, posterior samples, scripts, and public inputs.

Scope: the published evidence values use public frequency-by-frequency KDE products. The repository also contains a direct timing-likelihood extension, but its exploratory outputs are not the evidence values reported in the paper.

Frequently asked questions

What is the main conclusion?

Current public NANOGrav spectral evidence favors non-canonical structure over the strict scale-free SMBHB prediction. The result directs source interpretation toward environmental binary evolution or a spectrum with an early-Universe characteristic scale.

Which physical model is preferred?

The first-order phase-transition template has the largest compressed evidence among the tested physical templates. Environmental SMBHB hardening is the strongest astrophysical model. This ranking is conditional on the tested models, public KDE compression, and stated priors.

Does the analysis prove an early-Universe phase transition?

No. It shows that the tested phase-transition template explains the compressed spectrum better than the canonical scale-free SMBHB model under the baseline priors. Model ranking is not a unique source identification.

Where are the paper, data, and code?

The public GitHub repository contains the Version of Record, graphical abstract, scripts, and numerical outputs. The data-only archive is preserved on Zenodo at DOI 10.5281/zenodo.20319210.
